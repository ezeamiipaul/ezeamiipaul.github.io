About Me

Hello! I am a Ph.D. student in the Computing and Information Sciences program at the Rochester Institute of Technology (RIT), advised by Dr. Kristen Shinohara. My research explores adaptive systems for neurodivergent workflows, with the goal of designing technologies that better align with the diverse cognitive patterns of neurodivergent individuals. I am also a member of the Center for Accessibility and Inclusion Research (CAIR) Lab where I engage in Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) and Accessibility research.

Research Interests

My research investigates how adaptive and context-aware technologies can support neurodivergent researchers, particularly Ph.D. students with ADHD, in managing time, tasks, and focus throughout their academic workflows. By examining how these students navigate unstructured research environments, I aim to inform the design of human-centered systems that foster accessibility, productivity, and well-being in higher education.
